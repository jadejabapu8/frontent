Malana Village

Malana is an ancient village in the state of Himachal Pradesh, India. It is famous for its distinct culture and the "Malana Cream" cannabis.

Location and Environment: Located in the Parvati Valley of the Kullu district, Malana lies at an altitude of 2,652 meters. Surrounded by snow-clad mountains and thick deodar forests, it offers a surreal view of nature.

Cultural Significance: The people of Malana speak Kanashi, a language found nowhere else. They believe themselves to be descendants of Alexander the Great's soldiers and follow strict local customs.

Tourist Attraction: Popular among trekkers and nature lovers, the village remains one of the most isolated places in India. Tourists visit for the serene vibe and unique lifestyle.
